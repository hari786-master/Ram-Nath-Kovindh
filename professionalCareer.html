Footprints of Growth

1971-1976

Early Legal Career

From 1971 to 1976, Ram Nath Kovind practiced law in Delhi after enrolling with the Bar Council of Delhi. He worked in the Delhi High Court and provided legal services to ordinary clients. During this period, he was also associated with the Free Legal Aid Society, offering assistance to underprivileged groups, especially women and the poor. During this time, he also served as a standing counsel for the Union Government in the Delhi High Court. His dedication to justice and social welfare earned him respect within the legal community.

1977

Central Government Advocate

From 1977, Ram Nath Kovind served as a Central Government Advocate in the Delhi High Court. He represented the Union Government in various services and civil matters. His role involved drafting and presenting arguments in writ petitions and departmental disputes. He was also entrusted with handling sensitive cases that required thorough legal expertise. His work during this period strengthened his reputation as a diligent and reliable lawyer.

1978-1979

Advocate-on-record

In 1978, Ram Nath Kovind qualified as an Advocate-on-Record with the Supreme Court of India, which authorized him to file and plead cases directly before the Court. He represented both private clients and the Union Government in constitutional, service, and civil matters. His responsibilities included preparing petitions, drafting affidavits, and ensuring compliance with procedural rules of the Supreme Court. Alongside, he continued to provide pro bono assistance through the Free Legal Aid Society to marginalized groups.

1980-1993

Standing Counsel for the Union Government

From 1980 to 1993, Ram Nath Kovind served as Standing Counsel for the Union Government in the Supreme Court of India. In this capacity, he represented various ministries and departments of the Government of India in constitutional, civil, and service-related cases. He was responsible for preparing written submissions, drafting counter-affidavits, and presenting oral arguments before constitutional benches and regular courts. Kovind also dealt with cases involving government policies, administrative actions, and disputes affecting public servants.

1994

Entry into Parliament as Rajya Sabha Member

In 1994, Ram Nath Kovind was elected as a Member of the Rajya Sabha from Uttar Pradesh. As a parliamentarian, he participated in debates, raised questions, and contributed to discussions on national policies. He worked on issues concerning education, social justice, and the upliftment of marginalized communities. Kovind also served on various parliamentary committees, where he reviewed bills and policies affecting governance and development.

1994-2006

Ram Nath Kovind’s Two Terms in the Rajya Sabha

Ram Nath Kovind served two consecutive six-year terms in the Rajya Sabha from 1994 to March 2006 as a representative of Uttar Pradesh. During this period, he actively participated in parliamentary debates and raised concerns on matters of national and social importance. He was a member of several important parliamentary committees, including those on Welfare of Scheduled Castes/Tribes, Home Affairs, Petroleum and Natural Gas, Law and Justice, and Social Justice and Empowerment. Kovind consistently voiced issues related to education, the rights of Dalits, and opportunities for marginalized sections of society.

2015-2017

Contributions as Governor of Bihar

Ram Nath Kovind, as Governor of Bihar (2015–2017), focused on education, women’s empowerment, and social welfare. He actively supported state and central government development schemes and encouraged economic growth and employment opportunities. He emphasized good governance, transparency, and effective implementation of welfare programs. Kovind engaged with citizens through district visits and public outreach, promoting social harmony and cultural heritage. He also played a constitutional role in legislative matters, ensuring bills and policies complied with the law.

2017

Early Work as President of India

Ram Nath Kovind assumed office as the 14th President of India on 25 July 2017, becoming the first Dalit President from Uttar Pradesh. He emphasized social justice, education, and empowerment of marginalized communities. He supported the implementation of national welfare schemes and ensured constitutional compliance in legislation. Kovind promoted national integration, communal harmony, and India’s cultural heritage through speeches and public engagements. He also represented India globally, strengthening the country’s diplomatic relations and international image.

2023

Chairperson of the "One Nation, One Election" Panel

In September 2023, former President Ram Nath Kovind was appointed chairperson of a high-level committee to examine the feasibility of "One Nation, One Election." The panel studied the possibility of synchronizing elections for the Lok Sabha, state assemblies, and local bodies to reduce costs and administrative disruptions. After extensive consultations, the committee submitted a detailed report in March 2024, recommending constitutional amendments to enable simultaneous elections. The report sparked discussions among policymakers, political parties, and the public about its implications for governance and federalism. Kovind’s leadership of the panel marked a significant step toward exploring electoral reforms to streamline India’s democratic process.
